I’m a researcher in the field of Computer Vision and Machine Learning, employed as Post-Doc at the University of Trento (Italy).

Short Bio Paolo Rota has received his PhD in Information and Communication Technologies from the University of Trento in 2015. After 2 years as Marie Curie Fellow at TU Wien (Austria) in Computer Vision Lab (CVL), he joined the Pattern Analysis and Computer Vision (PAVIS) group at the Istituto Italiano di Tecnologia (IIT) in Genova. His research interests are in the field of Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition, spanning from machine learning, deep learning applied to scene understanding and image forensics. He is a member of the program committee of various prestigious conferences in the field (e.g.: CVPR, ECCV, ICCV, etc.).
He is also co-organizer of international workshops such as Multimodal Machine Learning and Applications (MULA 2018) that will be held in Munich during ECCV 2018 and Data-Driven Treatment Response Assessment Workshop (DATRA 2018) that will be held in Grenada in conjunction with MICCAI 2018.
